# Research notes: US Olive Oil Sourcing / Supply
Compiled 2026-07-23 by research agent. Raw material for the site.

## Where grown/milled; scale vs consumption
- California = >95% of US olive oil; ~84% of US olive acreage. CA processed >1.94M gallons oil in 2023.
- Other states marginal: AZ, TX, GA, FL, OR, HI. Georgia emerging; Arizona mill medaled 2024 NYIOOC; southern Oregon small craft.
- ~40,000 acres planted exclusively for oil (AOOPA).
- Shift canning->crushing: pre-2000s ~90% canned; by 2022 >75% crushed for oil (USDA ERS).
- US output ~12,000 MT in MY2023/24 (from ~2,000 MT early 2000s). CA ~21M lb (~2.5M gal) 2021-23 avg.
- US consumption ~90M gallons/yr (largest market outside EU). Imports supply >98% (domestic <2% per ERS; AOOPA says ~5%). Present 2-5% span; ERS more authoritative.

## Producers/mills for bulk
- California Olive Ranch (COR): top US supermarket EVOO, ~35% US output. Grower Private Label EVOO 500mL; 2L bag-in-box bulk/foodservice.
- Cobram Estate / Boundary Bend (US): Australian-owned, Woodland CA since 2014. COMPLETED acquisition of COR on 2026-03-26 (US$173.5M) => now largest US producer. Groves ~1,422 -> ~3,292 ha. CONSOLIDATION reduces independent supply/leverage.
- Corto Olive (Lodi): foodservice; 10L/20L FlavorLock boxes. Contact sales.
- Séka Hills (Yocha Dehe Wintun Nation, Capay Valley): estate EVOO 1,000+ stores; wholesale program.
- Bariani (Sacramento), McEvoy Ranch (Marin): premium estate; NO confirmed bulk/private-label — must inquire; do not assert.
- Bulk/co-pack/private-label (best for startups):
  - Desert Milling (Imperial Valley): CA EVOO milled in-house, no brokers; private-label+co-pack 250mL -> 5L BIB -> bulk totes; "flexible minimums"; ships unlabeled or fully labeled.
  - Sutter Buttes Olive Oil Co: private label; 2.5/5/55 gal.
  - Wild Groves: 2.5 gal jugs, 55-gal drums, 265/330-gal totes.
  - Sonoma Farm: CA EVOO 55-gal drums (208L), incl organic.
  - PressPack Olive Industries: 55-gal & 200L drums, 1000L IBC totes; private labeling.
  - Centra Foods: 55-gal drums, 275-gal totes; custom pricing.

## How a small buyer sources
- Container sizes: 2.5-5 gal jugs -> 10L/20L bag-in-box -> 55-gal drum (~208L) -> 265-330 gal IBC tote (~1000L) -> flexitank (~21,000 kg = full 20ft container).
- Two paths: (1) buy bulk drums/totes and bottle yourself; (2) co-packer/private-label fills+labels+case-packs under your brand (lowest overhead for mom-and-pop).
- MOQs almost never published — always request quote. Desert Milling advertises flexible minimums. Drum (55 gal) common practical minimum.
- Vetting: California Olive Oil Council (COOC) certification seal + member/mill directory.

## Realities/challenges of 100% US-grown
- Supply structurally tiny (<2% of consumption); premium CA oil largely committed to producers' own brands.
- Cobram-COR merger (Mar 2026) concentrates supply.
- Domestic costs more (land/water/labor). But imports spiked to record ~$8,310/MT MY2023/24, narrowing gap temporarily.
- Strong seasonality: single harvest Oct-Dec; freshest post-crush; plan volume against fixed annual pool. Bag-in-box/FlavorLock/drums protect freshness.
- Yield volatility: weather, alternate-bearing, drought, labor. CA volumes swing year to year.
