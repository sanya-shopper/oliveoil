# Research notes: Marketing, Distribution & Current Players
Compiled 2026-07-23 by research agent. Market sizes are estimates; vary by firm.

## Market size & growth
- US olive oil market ~$2.99-3.2B in 2024 (Grand View $3.13B). CAGR ~7-8% to early 2030s (IMARC 7.73% to $6.3B by 2033; GVR 7.4%; R&M 8.14% to $6.04B by 2033).
- US consumption ~283,000 tonnes 2024; ~278,000 t imported (Italy, Spain, Tunisia). Largest importer, small producer.
- Per-capita ~1 L/person/yr US vs ~14 L Spain, ~11 L Italy, Greece highest (10-20x). Household penetration ~50% (up from ~30% five yrs earlier).
- Drivers: health/Mediterranean-diet, premiumization to EVOO, organic/non-GMO, e-commerce/DTC.

## Distribution channels for a small brand
- UNFI + KeHE gate national retail; minimums, onboarding fees, 6-12 mo onboarding. Barriers for brands <~$1M.
- Slotting/shelf fees ~$50-300/SKU/store; up to $250-1,000/item national; chain-wide auth $5,000-75,000/SKU. Often "free fill" (1-3 cases) instead.
- Margin stack: brokers 5-10% gross; distributors ~20-30%; KeHE min 15% off-invoice on new items. Plus charge-backs, setup, spoilage.
- Own higher-margin channels: DTC e-commerce, tasting room, farmers markets. Target 40-60% direct. DTC runs 15-30% higher margin.
- NY foodservice: Baldor Specialty Foods (Hunts Point Bronx, ~$740M rev, 225+ trucks, overnight NY/Boston/DC) — Northeast on-ramp. Hudson Valley has multiple olive oil tasting shops.
- Oil-and-vinegar tasting-room model: refillable by the ounce from "fusti," paid tastings/classes (highest margin), adjacencies. Store margins ~10-30%.

## Current players
- CONSOLIDATION: Cobram Estate Olives (ASX:CBO, parent Boundary Bend) acquiring California Olive Ranch for US$173.5M ($88.5M cash + $70M vendor notes + $15M earn-out). CA groves ~1,422 -> ~3,292 ha; COR contracts 2,500+ ha. Synergies $12M FY27 -> $20M+/yr FY30. Cobram now dominant in US-grown EVOO.
- Cobram/Boundary Bend: FY2024 EBITDA $66.7M, op cash flow $64.1M. 1H FY25 oil sales +13.5% to $123.5M, EBITDA +75.4% to $14.5M. US Cobram-branded sales +103.4% 1H FY25. ~$270M FY25 rev guidance. Raised $115.6M Sept 2025 for US expansion.
- California Olive Ranch: largest US-grown brand; acquired Lucini Italia 2015; 2025 NYIOOC top US producer, 4th worldwide. (~$100M rev / ~9-12% share / "~60% domestic production" = unverified secondary estimates.)
- Graza: DTC breakout. Launched Jan 2022 (Brooklyn, single-source Spanish, Sizzle/Drizzle squeeze bottles). ~$48M rev 2024, projecting ~triple (~$144M) 2025. 5th-largest national brand (Nielsen); ~24% of category growth 2025; 11,000+ retailers on <$3M raised.
- Séka Hills (Yocha Dehe Wintun Nation, Capay Valley): 82 -> ~500 acres, own mill since 2012; ~13 NYIOOC awards; Native-owned provenance.
- McEvoy Ranch (Petaluma): 550-acre certified-organic estate, 30+ yrs, independent. Corto (100% CA, foodservice + limited retail). Bariani (unfiltered CA). Enzo's Table (award-winning CA family).
- Big importers dominate supermarket volume: Deoleo USA (Bertolli/Carapelli/Carbonell), Pompeian (cited best-selling US brand), Filippo Berio, Colavita, Star, Goya, Sovena.
- FLAG (contested): a 2026 retailer-blog review flagged COR among highest phthalate levels — single secondary source, unconfirmed.

## Marketing angles that work
- Awards, esp. NYIOOC (New York Int'l Olive Oil Competition): world's largest/most respected; 2025 awarded 742 oils (517 Gold, 225 Silver); US producers 92 awards (CA 81). Elevates small family producers. Ownable credibility.
- Harvest date & freshness: counters "rancid supermarket oil"; justifies premium.
- Provenance/story: single-estate/varietal, named grower, stewardship (Séka Hills, McEvoy). Local/upstate-NY identity as differentiator — but represent CA sourcing HONESTLY.
- Health/Mediterranean-diet framing + polyphenol/quality messaging.
- Experiential + DTC: tastings, classes, subscriptions ("adopt-a-tree"), format innovation (Graza squeeze, cook vs finish).
