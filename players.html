The Players

You'll share the shelf with an Australian-owned giant, a Brooklyn-born squeeze-bottle upstart, a handful of estate artisans, and the big importers that quietly own most of the aisle. Knowing how each is doing tells you where the open ground is.

The headline: the two biggest US brands are now one company

The most important recent event in US-grown olive oil is a merger. In a deal agreed in late 2025 and completed in March 2026, ASX-listed Cobram Estate Olives (parent of Boundary Bend) acquired California Olive Ranch — the top-selling brand of California-produced EVOO — for US$173.5 million 1. The combination expands Cobram's California planted groves from about 1,422 to 3,292 hectares and makes it, by a wide margin, the dominant force in American olive oil 1.

Why a small buyer should care Consolidation concentrates the domestic bulk supply and pricing power under one owner (see Sourcing). It also validates the category — big money believes US olive oil is a growth story.

Cobram Estate / Boundary Bend & California Olive Ranch

Cobram Estate (ASX: CBO)

Vertically integrated, milling in Woodland, CA since 2014. Publicly traded, and growing fast in the US.

  • FY2024 EBITDA A$66.7M; record operating cash flow 2
  • 1H FY2025 olive-oil sales +13.5%; US Cobram-branded sales +103% 2
  • Raised A$115.6M in Sept 2025 for US expansion 2

California Olive Ranch (COR)

Historically the largest US-grown olive-oil brand; earlier acquired Lucini Italia (2015). Now Cobram-owned.

  • Top US producer and 4th worldwide at the 2025 NYIOOC 1
  • Runs a grower private-label program useful to small buyers (see Sourcing)
  • Widely-cited ~$100M revenue / ~9–12% share figures are unverified secondary estimates — the company was private pre-acquisition.

Graza — proof that a newcomer can break through

The most encouraging story for a startup isn't a grower at all — it's a brand. Graza, launched January 2022 out of Brooklyn with single-source Spanish oil in two squeeze bottles ("Sizzle" to cook, "Drizzle" to finish), reportedly did about $48M in revenue in 2024 and projected to roughly triple gross sales in 2025 3. By Nielsen it became a top-five national olive-oil brand and reportedly drove around a quarter of the category's US growth in 2025 — expanding to 11,000+ retailers on under $3M raised 3.

The lesson, not the copy You won't out-Graza Graza. But it proves that format innovation, a clear use-case, freshness, and a strong DTC engine can beat incumbents with a fraction of their money. That's a small brand's real playbook.

The estate artisans — your true peer group

Séka Hills

Owned by the Yocha Dehe Wintun Nation (Capay Valley, CA). Scaled from 82 to ~500 acres with its own mill since 2012; ~13 NYIOOC awards in recent years. A masterclass in provenance-driven branding 4.

McEvoy Ranch

Family-owned, 550-acre certified-organic estate near Petaluma; 30+ years and still independent 5.

Corto · Bariani · Enzo's

100% California producers spanning foodservice (Corto), unfiltered family oil (Bariani), and award-winning estate bottles (Enzo's) 1.

The big importers — who really owns the shelf

Most supermarket volume still belongs to large importers, and their commodity positioning is exactly what a fresh, local brand differentiates against 6:

  • Deoleo (Bertolli, Carapelli, Carbonell)
  • Pompeian
  • Filippo Berio
  • Colavita
  • Star
  • Goya
  • Sovena

Individual current market shares aren't publicly broken out in the sources reviewed; Pompeian is frequently cited as the best-selling brand in the US 6.

A cautionary note on trust A 2026 independent-testing writeup flagged one major domestic brand for high phthalate levels. It comes from a single secondary source and is contested/unconfirmed — but it's a reminder that purity and quality claims are a live consumer-trust battleground, and a small brand's transparency can be a genuine advantage.

What the board tells you

  • The volume game is over — Cobram and the importers own it. Don't play it.
  • The premium, story, and freshness game is wide open — that's where Graza, Séka Hills, and McEvoy live, and where you'd compete.
  • DTC + format + provenance beats money more often than you'd think in this category.
  • Consolidation means you should lock in bulk supply relationships early and keep a backup source.
The other half of the board These giants are only the top of the market. Beneath them is a long tail of family shops and micro-brands — the tasting rooms, agritourism mills, and subscription makers that are your actual peer group, not your competition with a national salesforce.
